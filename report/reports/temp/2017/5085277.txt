Outbreak News Today [edited]<http://outbreaknewstoday.com/nigeria-reports-additional-468-meningitis-cases-16009/>Since our last report on May 22 [2017], nearly 2 weeks ago, Nigerian health officials have reported an additional 468 meningococcal meningitis cases, including 44 deaths.According to the Nigeria Centre for Disease Control (NCDC), from the beginning of the outbreak on [13 Dec 2016] to [2 Jun 2017], a total of 14 473 suspected cases and 1155 deaths (CFR [case fatality rate] of 8 percent) of meningitis have been reported from 25 States.As many as 460 cases have been confirmed and 8 out of 10 confirmed cases were identified as _Neisseria meningitidis_ serogroup C.A second phase of reactive vaccination was completed in Zamfara state on [26 May 2017] with 97 percent coverage and in Yobe state on [ 24 May 2017] with 105 percent coverage.Meningococcal meningitis is a bacterial form of meningitis, a serious infection of the thin lining that surrounds the brain and spinal cord and can cause severe brain damage. Meningococcal meningitis is fatal in [greater than] 50 percent of cases if untreated.Though cases of meningitis occur throughout the world, large, recurring epidemics affect an extensive region of sub-Saharan Africa known as the ""meningitis belt"" which includes 26 countries from Senegal in the west to Ethiopia in the east.[Byline: Robert Herriman]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[Discussions of the current meningococcal meningitis outbreak in Nigeria can be found in the following prior ProMED-mail posts:Meningitis, meningococcal - Nigeria (04): mass vaccination, comments 20170403.4944602Meningitis, meningococcal - Nigeria (03): fatal, more states, vaccine short, RFI 20170402.4942532Meningitis, meningococcal - Nigeria (02): fatal, emergence sg C, WHO 20170325.4925882Meningitis, meningococcal - Nigeria: (SO, ZA) fatal, new sg.susp., RFI 20170318.4909903. - Mod.MLA HealthMap/ProMED-mail map can be accessed at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/62>.]
