Nagaland Post [edited]<https://www.nagalandpost.com/ChannelNews/Regional/RegionalNews.aspx?news=TkVXUzEwMDExODM3MQ%3D%3D>While officials of the state health department are busy combating outbreaks of diseases like swine flu, Japanese encephalitis, and dengue, scrub typhus has claimed one life in Manipur.A 4-year-old [has] also tested positive for scrub typhus barely 2 days after the disease had claimed a 21-year-old youth in Manipur Imphal West district on [24 Jul 2017]. The 4-year-old boy, currently [being treated] at a private hospital, [has] tested positive for scrub typhus. The boy started suffering from high fever on [21 Jul 2017] and he was admitted to a private hospital at Sagolband in Imphal for treatment. The serological test confirmed as scrub typhus case. The child is a resident of Pukhao village in Imphal but staying at his aunt's residence at Thangmeiband in Imphal. It is believed the kid [contracted] the infection when he was playing at Cheiraoching in Thangmeiband about 10 days back.Following the confirmation of the child suffering from the disease, a team of medical officers of state health services rushed to the hospital and observed the condition of the child. According to the doctors attending him, the condition of the child was improving slowly and his life is out of risk. The disease can be cured if treated timely. There is no need for [panic] by the people. The disease is curable though it [is] dangerous if [treatment is delayed], the doctors opined.Scrub typhus, an acute fever-causing infectious illness caused by a bacterium called _Orientia tsutsugamushi_, has claimed [the] life of a youth who was staying at a flood camp at Sekmaijing Mamang Leikai in Imphal West district. The victim identified was suffering from fever from [12 Jul 2017], when he was in the flood relief camp at Sekmaijing.The outbreak of the disease [occurs] at a time when the people of [the] state are facing outbreaks of other diseases like swine flu [influenza A(H1N1)], dengue, and Japanese encephalitis. [To] date, 38 cases of dengue, 30 cases of Japanese encephalitis, and one [?] were confirmed this summer [2017]. A majority of the cases were reported from Churachandpur district.
