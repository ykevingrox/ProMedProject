.0,7855926.0,2017-08-27 05:28:25,United States,United States,Not Yet Classified,Humans,?id=20170827.5276191,PRO/ALL> Obituary: Louisa Elaine Chapman,"OBITUARY: LOUISA ELAINE CHAPMAN*******************************A ProMED-mail post<http://www.promedmail.org>ProMED-mail is a program of theInternational Society for Infectious Diseases<http://www.isid.org>Date: 25 Aug 2017From: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention announcement [edited]It is with profound sadness that we announce the death of Louisa Elaine Chapman, MD, MSPH. Louisa was a medical epidemiologist and associate director for science in the Division of Public Health Information Dissemination (DPHID), Center for Surveillance, Epidemiology, and Laboratory Services (CSELS). She was also a captain in the United States Public Health Service.Louisa tirelessly served and promoted public health for more than 3 decades. She received her bachelor of arts degree in biology and philosophy from Macalester College in St Paul, Minnesota [United States]. She went on to receive her master of science in public health degree from the School of Public Health at the University of North Carolina in Chapel Hill, North Caroline, and her MD degree from the School of Medicine at the University of North Carolina. She was a diplomat of the American Board of Internal Medicine in Internal Medicine and Infectious Diseases.Prior to her work with CDC, Louisa worked as a physician scientist and researched chemical carcinogenesis. She was certified as a secondary science teacher, practiced geriatric and general medicine, and served as the medical director of a medium security men's work prison.Louisa joined CDC and the United States Public Health Service in 1988 as an epidemic intelligence service officer and investigated Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever and Rift Valley fever in Senegal and Lassa fever in Sierra Leone. In subsequent CDC assignments, she made significant contributions to CDCs' mission by advancing the science of infectious diseases and surveillance and serving in various emergency responses. Throughout her nearly 30-year federal career, Louisa actively collaborated across CDC and the United States' public health community. She served in advisory and collaborative roles with the executive offices of the president of the United States, the US Department of Homeland Security, the Department of State, the Department of Defense, the National Academies of Science, Indian Health Service, and other entities within DHHS. She particularly enjoyed yearly visits to the Cherokee Indian Hospital in Cherokee, NC, where she provided clinical care to patients.Louisa was a widely published author with more than 140 scientific products. She served on the editorial board of Emerging Infectious Diseases journal and several other journals, chaired the publications committee of the Infectious Disease Society of America, and served as the vice chair of CDC's Surveillance Science Advisory Group (SurvSag).Louisa received numerous awards for her outstanding professional service and achievement. She was known worldwide as an expert in viral epidemiology and was an invited reviewer for and consultant to the US National Academies of Sciences, Department of Defense, DHHS National Institutes of Health, Food & Drug Administration, World Health Organization, Organization for Economic Cooperation & Development, and the health authorities of Canada, the United Kingdom, and the European Union. Louisa's scientific contributions to CDC were extensive and her contributions towards ensuring scientific quality, integrity, and growth made a lasting difference within the agency.A memorial service will be held on [Mon 28 August], at 10:00 am ET at the Cathedral of St Philip, 2744 Peachtree Road NW, Atlanta, Georgia 30305.--communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>.................................................mpp/sh/ao/sh"
