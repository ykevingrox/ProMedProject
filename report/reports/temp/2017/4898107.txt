The Standard, Xinhua News Agency report [edited]<http://www.thestandard.com.hk/breaking-news.php?id=86057>Local health authorities said 4 more human cases of [avian influenza A/H7N9] have been reported in eastern Jiangxi Province, including one fatality. The new cases brought the number of infections in the province this year [2017] to 36 and fatalities to 12, according to the provincial health and family planning commission. The province has increased supervision of local poultry markets. H7N9 is an avian influenza strain first reported to have infected humans in China in March 2013. Infections are most likely occur in winter and spring.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>******[2] ChongqingDate: Wed 15 Mar 2017 9:30 AM CSTSource: FIC (Flu Information Centre/Flu in China) [edited]<http://www.flu.org.cn/en/news_detail?action=ql&uid=MjI0OA&pd=YXRsbXBw&newsId=19158>Fengjie County of Chongqing city reported a human H7N9 case after confirmation test performed by CDC on [14 Mar 2017]. [The 43-year-old male patient who had history of contact with] poultry, remained in hospital for treatment. All close contacts of the patient showed no ILI [influenza-like-illness] symptoms.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[Maps of China can be seen at <http://www.sacu.org/maps/provmap.png> and <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/155>. - Sr.Tech.Ed.MJ]
