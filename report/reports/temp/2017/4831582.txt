Cases hit a 50-year high in 2015/16, and Public Health England say figures are on course for a similar rate this year [2017]. The peak season for cases is usually in March and April, and parents are encouraged to learn the symptoms now.In its latest report, Public Health England said rates of notified cases so far this season were highest in the East Midlands (8.6 per 100 000 people), followed by the North West (7.7), Yorkshire & Humber (7.5) and the North East (6.9).--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[The scarlet fever outbreak that has recurred throughout England in 2014, 2015, and 2016, has been worse than at any time in the past 50 years (<http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/health-and-families/health-news/fastest-rise-in-scarlet-fever-cases-for-half-a-century-10045621.html>).
