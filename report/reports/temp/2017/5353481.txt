Tokyo Reporter [edited]<http://www.tokyoreporter.com/2017/10/01/87-kindergartners-fall-ill-in-aichi-over-salmonella-tainted-bento-meals/>Nearly 100 children at 4 kindergartens in Okazaki City fell ill after eating boxed meals tainted with _Salmonella_ prepared by a company with a history of providing tainted meals.A total of 87 kindergartners, aged 3 to 6, reported symptoms like fever and vomiting after consuming the tainted bento meals between 21 and 28 Sep 2017, according to officials of Okazaki, reports TBS News on 29 Sep 2017. _Salmonella_ was detected in many of the affected children, 10 of whom were hospitalized, but none are in life-threatening condition, authorities said.The tainted bento meals were provided to the kindergartens in the cities of Nagoya, Gamagori, and Nisshin by Tokiwa Shyokuhin foods in Okazaki, city officials said. Officials of Okazaki said Tokiwa Shyokuhin was suspended from business operations amid fears there could be more victims. The food company was also responsible for producing meals that left 71 people with food poisoning in April 2016, according to the Asahi Shimbun.The head of one of the affected kindergartens said, ""There's some anger here in light of the incident, which resulted from [the company] we trusted.""[Byline: Roland Shichijo]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[The food implicated in the transmission of salmonellosis in the boxed meals is not stated. Undercooked chicken or eggs are possible vehicles, but salmonellae can be found in many foods.
