Dengue Outbreak Emergency Plan of Action Final Report from International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies. Between January and May 2016, Seychelles experienced an exponential increase in the number of confirmed cases of dengue fever. The outbreak was in all 5 regions of the country, with approximately 253 people tested positive for dengue as from January up till 21 May 2016, as reported by the Ministry of Health. More specifically, there was an exponential increase in the number of confirmed cases from week 16 onwards with the peak (66 cases) in week 19. In total 175 males and 96 females tested positive.As of 12 July 2016, the Ministry of Health confirmed the total number of cases as 1062, with the outbreak extending to all 5 regions of the country, the 5 most affected districts being La Riviere Anglaise, (101 cases), Anse Royal (99 cases), Anse Etoile (82 cases), Beau Vallon (78 cases) and Point La Rue (62 cases).
