An estimated 1.5 million persons lived in endemic zones during the 1980's. For several decades, disease rates in Tanzania have paralleled those reported for Africa as a whole [2] (see graph at <https://www.gideononline.com/wp/wp-content/uploads/Trypanosomiais-Tanzania.png>)Prevalence surveys have demonstrated that 15.8 percent of cattle (_Bos indicus_) from traditional pastoral Maasai and managed Boran regions are infested; 0.7 percent in Monduli District, northern Tanzania. The parasite has also been identified in 10.1 percent of domestic pigs in Mbulu, Arumeru and Dodoma; and in 0.01 percent of tsetse flies (_Glossina swynnertoni_ and _G. pallidipes_) in Serengeti National Park.References:1.
