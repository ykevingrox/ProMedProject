Pakistan Today [edited]<https://www.pakistantoday.com.pk/2017/09/08/chikungunya-patients-in-tharparkar-exceed-2500/>Chikungunya patients have exceeded 2500 in number as more were tested positive on [Fri 8 Sep 2017].District Health Officer (DHO) Dr Shafique Memon said that the chikungunya outbreak has spread to several villages of Islamkot in Tharparkar. He added that all out efforts were being put in place to overcome the disease, and all resources were being utilized to treat the affected.Dr Memon also urged the people of the affected areas to adopt the precautionary measures issued by the health department to keep them safe from the disease.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[The 1st chikungunya outbreak in Pakistan was reported in Karachi during December 2016 and has spread to other parts of the country, including Tharparkar. The case counts of chikungunya in the Tharparkar district of Sindh have exceeded 2500 as of [Fri 8 Sep 2017], according to health officials. The news report also indicates that several villages are affected in Islamkot Taluka (Tehsil) of the district.There is need to enhance vector surveillance, as well as for human cases, and implementation of integrated vector management programs for the prevention and control of the disease to prevent its spread to other districts of the province. Management of human cases is generally supportive and no vaccination is available. - Mod.UBAA HealthMap/ProMED-mail map can be accessed at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/140>.]
