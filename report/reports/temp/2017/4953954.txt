Daily Post [edited]<http://dailypost.ng/2017/04/05/meningitis-outbreak-hits-cross-river-one-person-confirmed-dead/>Cerebral meningitis outbreak has reportedly hit Cross River State with one person confirmed dead.Commissioner of Health in the state, Dr Inyang Asibong, addressing newsmen in Calabar (Capital of Cross River state), on [Wed 5 Apr 2017], shortly after visiting some victims at University of Calabar Teaching Hospital, said about 24 cases have been reported with one confirmed dead.The Commissioner said the ministry has put facilities and personnel in place to reduce the impact of the disease, adding that most of the victims have been treated and discharged from the hospital. She added that the death toll would have been heavier if not for the prompt action of her ministry to contain its outbreak.Asibong said only about 3 people were currently undergoing treatment at the University of Calabar Teaching Hospital (UCTH) just as the situation in the state is calm.She said, ""We have recorded one death but we have tried to ensure that the situation is contained, that is why we have put in place facilities and personnel to ensure that as much as possible, we do not record more cases of death.""The ministry has started sensitizing the public on the dangers of meningitis in particular and other communicable diseases in general. Besides, the state government has given the ministry the needed support to contain the situation.""We are fighting the outbreak of the disease with everything within our power to ensure that the disease is contained as soon as possible. We in the ministry are mobilizing to drastically reduce the Impact of the disease, so we are doing something about the situation.""[Byline: John Owen Nwachukwu]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[A summary of the ongoing meningococcal meningitis serogroup C outbreak in Nigeria can be found in item 6 of the prior ProMED-mail post Meningitis, meningococcal - Nigeria (04): mass vaccination, comments 20170403.4944602.Cross River is a coastal state in southeastern Nigeria, bordering Cameroon to the east; its capital is Calabar (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_River_State>).
