Sud Ouest [in French, trans Corr.SB, edited]<http://www.sudouest.fr/2017/09/08/une-poussee-d-hepatite-a-3758646-6540.php>Since the beginning of 2017, France is experiencing an upsurge in cases of hepatitis A, a viral disease that attacks liver cells, the most common of which is benign. But this infection can make you ill for weeks or even months.In Gironde [Nouvelle-Aquitaine region], 66 cases have been diagnosed since the beginning of 2017. It is the most affected region and has experienced a cluster of 16 people who ate at a restaurant in Bordeaux [Gironde department].[Byline: Elisa Artigue-Cazcarra]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[ProMED would appreciate information regarding risk factors in these cases, that is, whether they are MSM, household case contacts, travel to high risk area, etc.Gironde is a department in the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region of southwest France.
