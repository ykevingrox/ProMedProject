New Straights Times [edited]<https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2017/07/259497/malaysia-indonesia-form-joint-committee-contain-rabies-outbreak-along>Malaysia and Indonesia have agreed to form a joint committee on prevention and control of rabies outbreak along the border between Sarawak and West Kalimantan, Indonesia.The formation of the joint committee was deliberated and agreed upon during a special meeting between delegates from the state government led by Deputy Chief Minister Datuk Douglas Uggah Embas and representatives from the Indonesian authorities.The secretariat in a statement said the committee would be jointly headed by Deputy State Secretary (Rural Development) Datuk Jaul Simon representing Sarawak and Indonesian consul-general in Kuching, Muhammad Abdullah.""The committee will promote greater cooperation among health and security workers from Sarawak and Indonesia for better prevention and mitigation efforts to curb the spread of the virus.""""Both Sarawak and Indonesia will exchange information and expertise on ways on rabies prevention and control.""""Both parties will conduct stricter enforcement along official and unofficial border to prevent migration of dogs,"" the secretariat said today [Fri 21 Jul 2017].The meeting was held in the wake of the rabies outbreak in the Serian district, believed to be caused by dogs infected [with] the virus from West Kalimantan.Since the outbreak emerged in the district in June [2017], the virus has claimed the lives of 4 children aged between 4 and 7. The 5th confirmed case involved a 52-year-old man, who had history of being bitten by a dog.[Byline: Adib Povera]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[Malaysia has submitted to the OIE on [Thu 20 Jul 2017], its follow-up report No. 1 addressing the rabies event in animals, which reportedly had started on Sarawak (its Western territory on the Borneo island, neighbouring Indonesia's West Kalimantan) on [Tue 4 Jul 2017].According to the Follow-up report, 9 new rabies outbreaks in animals have been recorded, bringing the total to 11.
