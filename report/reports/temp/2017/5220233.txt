UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA), ReliefWeb [edited]<http://reliefweb.int/report/world/pacific-syndromic-surveillance-system-weekly-bulletin-syst-me-de-surveillance-13>The Ministry of Health and Medical Services (MHMS) has been working closely with the Ministry of Education and World Health Organization (WHO) to address the recent outbreak of meningococcal disease in a boarding school in the Eastern health division [1].There have been 8 cases, of which are 4 are confirmed, including one  fatal case. Testing of suspected case samples, facilitated by WHO, at the Microbiological Diagnostic Unit, Public Health laboratory, University of Melbourne confirmed that meningococcal serogroup C is responsible for this outbreak. In response to the outbreak, the MHMS this week are vaccinating the school cohort with the meningococcal vaccine, Sanofi Pasteur Menactra. [Menactra is a quadrivalent meningococcal protein conjugate vaccine in which 4 meningococcal serogroup polysaccharides (A, C, Y, and W-135) are conjugated to diphtheria toxoid <https://www.fda.gov/downloads/biologicsbloodvaccines/vaccines/approvedproducts/ucm131170.pdf>. - Mod.ML][1.
