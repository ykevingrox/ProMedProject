Southport Visiter [summ., edited]<http://www.southportvisiter.co.uk/news/southport-west-lancs/warning-after-new-reported-cases-12380468>Health officials say there have been a number of new reported cases of scarlet fever in Sefton over the past few weeks. Figures issued by Public Health England suggest up to 8 new cases have been reported in the borough, since the beginning of December [2016].[Byline: Kate Lally]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[This past year the number of scarlet fever cases in England and Wales reached the highest level reported in 49 years (<https://www.gov.uk/government/news/update-on-rising-scarlet-fever-across-england>). The total number of cases reported so far this year (epidemiological weeks 1-51 of 2016) was 19 046, which is higher than that for the same period in the previous 2 years (15 468 cases in 2014 and 17 357 cases in 2015); the total number of cases of scarlet fever reported in England and Wales in epidemiological week 51 (18-24 Dec 2016) was 269, which was similar to that reported in the 2 preceding weeks (<https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/580835/NOIDS-weekly-report-week_51-2016.pdf>).Sefton, with a population of 273 790 residents, just north of the city of Liverpool, is a metropolitan borough of Merseyside, a metropolitan county in North West England (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Sefton>). - Mod.MLA HealthMap/ProMED-mail map can be accessed at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/48732>.]
