Daily Post [edited]<http://dailypost.ng/2016/02/20/fresh-disease-outbreak-kills-25-children-in-lekki/>Jide Idris, the Lagos state commissioner of health, has confirmed the death of 25 children as a result of an outbreak of ""febrile rash illness"" (FRI) in Otodo-Gbame community in Ikate, Lekki. At a news conference in Lagos, the commissioner confirmed that there were 34 cases of sick children with FRI and added that the graves of 20 children have been identified by their parents.""The graves of 20 children who died from the FRI since the day of onset of signs and symptoms in the 1st case on 6 Jan [2016] were identified by their parents. Also, 34 cases have been line listed so far with 17 males and 17 females; 17 dead patients have been identified with 9 males and 8 females, all are within the age range of 8 and 72 months,"" Idris said. ""The source of the infection is still under investigation and until we know the [specific] source, we cannot say for sure what is causing it.""The unknown disease, which started some time in January 2016, made the predominantly Egun community panic [the Egun are an ethnic group who make up about 15 per cent of the indigenous population of Lagos State in Nigeria - CopyEd.PG from Wikipedia]. After contracting the ailment, the affected children developed rashes similar to [that seen in] measles, and died 2-3 days afterwards.Idris said that the team of epidemiologists from the state ministry of health are yet to come up with the report on the source of the strange infection, but added that the signs and symptoms of the illness suggested febrile rash. ""Blood samples and throat swabs from the patients and water samples from the community have been taken to the Virology Reference Laboratory at Lagos University Teaching Hospital and Lagos State Drug Quality Control Laboratory and Lagos State University Teaching Hospital, Ikeja.""He, however, urged the citizenry to continue to practice health promotion and disease prevention measures. According to him, such measures should include basic environmental sanitation, including proper disposal of refuse, and avoiding open defecation, as well as regular handwashing with soap and water, maintaining personal hygiene, adequate nutrition, and antenatal care for pregnant women.He listed other measures as ""immunisation of children against deadly vaccine-preventable childhood diseases, and regular checkups towards ensuring early diagnosis and prompt treatment of both communicable and non-communicable diseases.""--communicated by:ProMED-mail and HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[Of course, vaccination is only effective when the etiologic agent is known and a vaccine exists. - Mod.LK]******[2] Comment: Undiagnosed illness - Nigeria: (LA) fatal, RFI 20160220.4035781:The disease is being called ""febrile rash illness"", but a precise diagnosis has not yet been made. 34 cases have been reported, aged 8-72 months, all in the Eti-Osa Local Government Area of Lagos State.
