Odisha Sun Times [edited]<http://odishasuntimes.com/2016/04/12/two-die-anthrax-odisha/>Anthrax has claimed 2 lives in a family in 2 days in Dangadhar village, Boipariguda block in Koraput district of Odisha. The deceased have been identified as [a female aged 45 years and her 12-year-old daughter]. According to reports, 4 other persons suffering from anthrax in the village are in a serious condition.A health team had visited the area yesterday [11 Apr 2016] and treated 2 persons.[Notably, the 1st week in April [2016] 4 people had died due to the dreaded bacterial infection in the Jambaguda village in Rayagada.]Anthrax is a rare disease caused by the spores of the bacterium _Bacillus anthracis_. It causes enlargement of the spleen and is also known as spleen fever or 'wool sorter's disease'. The bacterium is transmitted from animals to the humans and is caused by eating [contaminated] meat, according to experts.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>******[2]Date: Tue 12 Apr 2016Source: Prameya News 7 [edited]<http://prameyanews7.com/en/apr2016/odisha/20696/Odisha-Four-die-11-serious-due-to-anthrax-in-Koraput-Anthrax-in-KoraputOdisha-Odisha.htm>At least 4 persons have died and 11 others, including 5 seriously, have been affected by anthrax in Boipariguda area of Odisha's Koraput district.According to information, the disease attacked some villagers after they ate meat of dead goats and pigs. People in 2 villages, Kadamba and Dakadhar under the Chipakur gram panchayat [local self-governments at village or small town level] in the district, have been infected in the disease.A team of medical officers led by ADMO [additional district medical officer] Dr Arun Kumar Pani will visit the 2 villagers today [12 Apr 2016] to collect blood samples for test, sources said.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[Maps of India showing Odisha can be seen at <http://www.mapsofindia.com/maps/india/india-political-map.htm> and <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/1601>; for Boipariguda go to <http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/21/Boiparigurha.html>.The source of the human infection is claimed to be some dead pigs and goats; it is probable that the pigs became infected from eating a goat that had died of anthrax. The frequency of this disease, whether in livestock or in humans, would indicate that a statewide livestock vaccination programme is overdue. - Mod.MHJ]
