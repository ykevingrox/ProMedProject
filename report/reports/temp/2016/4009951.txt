The Age [edited]<http://www.theage.com.au/victoria/salmonella-outbreak-hits-100-cases-in-victoria-20160210-gmqlyz.html>The number of salmonellosis cases in Victoria associated with a pre-packed lettuce recall has reached 100. The health department's figure is almost double the running total as of 8 Feb 2016, when there had been 62 cases in the state.The recalled lettuce includes brands such as Coles 4 Leaf Mix, Woolworths salad mix, SupaSalad Supamix and Wash N Toss salad mix. They have best-before dates leading up to and including 14 Feb 2016. Since the recall was announced on 4 Feb 2016, a total of 128 salmonellosis cases have been reported nationwide.Almost all of the cases have been confirmed in Victoria; however, there have been 12 cases in South Australia, 8 in Queensland, 6 in New South Wales and 2 in Western Australia. Of the 128 cases, only 10 have been confirmed so far, but further testing is underway for the rest.[Byline: Craig Butt]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap alerts<http://www.promedmail.org>[The number of cases continues to rise as the time approaches the best-before date of 14 Feb 2016.
