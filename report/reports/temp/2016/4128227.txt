The Statesman, Statesman News Service (SNS) [edited]<http://www.thestatesman.com/news/odisha/200-fall-sick-due-to-food-poisoning/133156.html>At least 200 people of 4 villages under Tirtol police station limits, mostly minors fell ill, after consuming 'dahi bara' [dumplings made from split black gram beans and soaked in a yogurt sauce] from vendors. A medical team has rushed to the affected villages. The case of food poisoning was reported from Gopalpur, Putting, Jaisinghpur, Ainipur villages [Jagatsinghpur district, Odisha]..Nearly 200 people including 120 kids were taken ill after they consumed dahi bara from said seller. They complained of vomiting, stomach ache, and loose motions and many were rushed to Sanra Primary Health Centre for treatment. Some of the patients were shifted to the district headquarter hospital, Jagatsinghpur.50 persons including 35 children have been admitted in district hospital while other persons have been treated by medical team in their respective villages. Chief District Medical Officer (in charge) Dhaneswer Pradhan has expressed ""doctors, nurses have been alerted round the clock in district hospital for treatment of 50 patients who have been admitted in district hospital. It is a case of food poisoning,"" he said.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[The etiology of this foodborne illness cluster in the Indian state of Odisha is unclear, as viral, bacterial endotoxin, bacterial infection, parasitic, and chemical etiologies can be present.
