Lincolnshire Echo [edited]<http://www.lincolnshireecho.co.uk/new-scarlet-fever-cases-confirmed-in-lincolnshire/story-29642717-detail/story.html>Public Health England has confirmed that there were 4 new cases of scarlet fever in Lincolnshire in its latest report.For the week ending [14 Aug 2016], there were 3 cases in Lincoln and 1 case in West Lindsey.Nationally, the figures show there were 132 new cases of the bacterial illness -- which mainly affects children.[Byline: Caroline Wilson]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[As of 30 Apr 2016, a total of 12 906 scarlet fever notifications had been made, with notifications peaking at the end of March [2016] in England since the season began in September 2015; and the areas with the highest population rates to date are Devon, Cornwall & Somerset (3.9 per 100 000 population), Yorkshire & Humber (3.7/100 000), and the North East (<http://tinyurl.com/jtm44vu>). Lincolnshire, for which data are given in the news report above, is partly in the Yorkshire & Humber region and partly in the East Midlands region of England. A map of the regions of England can be found at <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regions_of_England>.For the same period in 2014-2015, there were 9379 cases (<https://www.gov.uk/government/news/update-on-rising-scarlet-fever-across-england>). 2015-2016 is the 3rd season in a row in which elevated scarlet fever activity has been noted.
