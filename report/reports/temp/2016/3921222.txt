The Poultry Site [edited] <http://www.thepoultrysite.com/poultrynews/36388/northern-southern-nigeria-both-hit-by-bird-flu-outbreaks/>Nigeria has reported more outbreaks of highly pathogenic avian influenza in its Kano and Delta regions.1 outbreak of the H5N1 strain on a farm in the Delta region caused 7973 deaths in a flock comprising broilers, layers and breeders at different ages of production.Over 330 000 poultry were destroyed as a result of the outbreak.In the Kano region, H5N1 outbreaks on 3 different farms caused the death of 376 birds, and another 15 000 had to be destroyed to prevent the disease spreading.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[Nigeria began undergoing 2 major HPAI H5N1 epizootics during 2015.The 1st reportedly started on 24 Dec 2014 in the southwestern part of the country, so far notified to the OIE in an immediate notification and 29 follow-up reports. All the reports and an interactive map showing all affected locations are available at <http://www.oie.int/wahis_2/public/wahid.php/Reviewreport/Review?page_refer=MapFullEventReport&reportid=19461>. The most recent follow-up report, No. 29, was submitted on 5 Jan 2016.The 2nd epizootic reportedly started on 2 Jan 2015, also in the southwestern part of the country, so far notified to the OIE in an immediate notification and 16 follow-up reports. All the reports and an interactive map showing all affected locations are available at <http://www.oie.int/wahis_2/public/wahid.php/Reviewreport/Review?page_refer=MapFullEventReport&reportid=19447>. The most recent follow-up report, No. 16, was submitted on 4 Jan 2016.Modified stamping out policy, without vaccination, is being applied.So far, no human infections have been reported. - Mod.ASA HealthMap/ProMED-mail map can be accessed at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/5476>.]
