The Nation [edited]<http://nation.com.pk/national/03-Sep-2016/congo-virus-patients-toll-hits-27-in-balochistan>A 12-year-old boy was diagnosed with Congo virus [Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic (CCHF) fever] today [3 Sep 2016], taking the total number of affectees in Balochistan to 27, with 10 dead so far.According to hospital sources, [the boy] was brought for a check-up 4 days ago after he started bleeding from his nose and mouth.Meanwhile, another patient, a 37-year-old from Shikarpur [Sindh] was admitted to Fatima Jinnah General and Chest Hospital [in Quetta, Balochistan] on suspicion of the same disease.The total number of people who were admitted to different hospitals for scan for the fatal virus in Balochistan stands at 94 out of which 27 were found positive.Yesterday [2 Sep 2016], one of the affected patients, a 55-year-old who was under treatment, died taking the death toll to 10 in Balochistan.Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever (CCHF) spreads through vectors that carry the virus found in cattle.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[CCHF presents a perfect example of emerging and reemerging zoonoses that threaten local, regional, and global health. The key to prevention and control is establishment of a sensitive and robust surveillance system using the One Health approach, so that transmission events can be detected at the earliest.
