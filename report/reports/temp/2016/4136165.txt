Wales Online [edited]<http://www.walesonline.co.uk/news/wales-news/farm-investigated-after-visitors-test-11119534>A Monmouthshire [Wales] farm is being investigated after a number of visitors tested positive for a microscopic parasite that causes a diarrheal disease.  Public Health Wales, Torfaen County Borough Council and Monmouthshire County Councilare investigating an outbreak of cryptosporidium at Coleg Gwent's farm in Usk. So far, 3 people have tested positive for cryptosporidium and 8 others are under investigation after a regular attendance at the farm.Doctors in the Gwent area have been told to alert people about the disease. Heather Lewis, a consultant in health protection for Public Health Wales, said: ""[Cryptosporidiosis] is a disease that is very common in young farm animals and can easily be passed to people who come into contact with those animals. ""All the confirmed cases had direct contact with the lambs at the college farm.
