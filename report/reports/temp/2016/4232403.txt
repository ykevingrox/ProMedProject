The latest report of the Directorate of National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP) reveals that a total of 99 913 cases and 220 deaths by the mosquito borne disease were reported in 2015. In Maharashtra state: the number of cases and deaths have dropped by half (4936 cases and 23 deaths). Delhi, Punjab, Haryana and West Bengal recorded maximum cases. Barring a few states like Maharashtra and Orissa, the rest of the states and union territories reported an increase in cases and deaths. Mortality rate declined from 1.2 percent in 2007 to 0.25 percent in 2013 and further to 0.22 percent in 2015. The Government of India has notified all states to observe 16 May as National Dengue Day to promote awareness and ensure public participation to curb transmission of dengue virus.<http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/health-fitness/health-news/All-out-fight-fails-to-tame-dengue-menace/articleshow/52304968.cms>[The HealthMap/ProMED map of India is available at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/142> - Mod.MPP]India. 14 May 2016. As many as 26 cases of dengue were reported in April [2016] against 4 cases reported in the same month last year [2015]. In the last 13 days, there were 9 cases of dengue. The number of cases is likely to increase with the onset of rain. District Malaria Officer Arun Kumar S.B. said that these cases were largely noticed in houses near arecanut plantations. No cases were reported from residential localities. Field staff and officials made farm owners destroy mosquito breeding sources, he said. Dr. Kumar said that they have undertaken fever survey and given treatment to those suspected of having viral infection.
