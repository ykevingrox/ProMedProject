And there were 3 in East Lindsey, 2 in South Kesteven and 1 each in Lincoln, West Lindsey and Boston.[Byline: CJ Hall]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[As of 27 Mar 2016, Public Health England (PHE) reported a total of 10 570 cases of scarlet fever since the season began in September 2015. An increase in invasive disease caused by the same bacterium group A streptococcus (GAS) which causes scarlet fever has also been seen in England with a total of 593 cases of invasive GAS infection, such and bloodstream infection or pneumonia, reported for 2016 compared to 440 cases for the same period last year (Jan to Mar 2015).PHE reported that this is the third year in a row in which elevated scarlet fever activity has been noted. A total of 15 637 notifications were made in England and Wales in 2014, rising to 17 590 in 2015.
