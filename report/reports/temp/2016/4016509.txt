Saudi Arabia Ministry of Health [edited]<http://www.moh.gov.sa/en/CCC/PressReleases/Pages/Statistics-2016-02-12-001.aspx>As of 12:00 noon today [12 Feb 2016] there have been a total of:1294 laboratory-confirmed cases of MERS-CoV infection including552 deaths [reported case fatality rate 42.7 percent]739 recoveries, and3 currently active casesInformation on newly reported cases today:1- A 34-year-old non-healthcare worker expat male from Najran who was reported as having died at the time of initial confirmation. Information on possible risk exposures is currently under investigation. He did not have a history of pre-existing co-morbidities.2- A 41-year-old non-healthcare worker Saudi male from Alkharj [in Riyadh province] who was noted to be in stable condition at the time of initial confirmation. The patient was noted to be a household contact of someone with known contact with camels (hence having indirect contact with camels).In the interval since the last newly reported case on 3 Feb 2016 there have been 6 additional reported recoveries, 2 of whom had been reported as asymptomatic infections [see <http://www.moh.gov.sa/en/CCC/PressReleases/Pages/default.aspx?PageIndex=1>]. The 6 recoveries were:1- On 5 Feb 2016 a 43-year-old non-healthcare worker expat male from Riyadh who had been reported as a new stable case on 1 Feb 2016 at which time possible risk exposures were under investigation.2- On 6 Feb 2016 a 47-year-old non-healthcare worker Saudi male from Riyadh who had been reported as a new stable case from Alkharj on 27 Jan 2016 at which time he was noted to be a primary case with direct exposure to camels. 3- On 7 Feb 2016 a 50-year-old non-healthcare worker Saudi male from Madinah who had been reported as a new stable case on 13 Jan 2016 at which time he was noted to be a primary case with direct exposure to camels. 4- On 8 Feb 2016 a 21-year-old non-healthcare worker expat male from Jeddah who had been reported as an asymptomatic infection from Alkhumra on 24 Jan 2016 at which time he was noted to have had direct exposure to camels. 5- On 8 Feb 2016 a 45-year-old non-healthcare worker expat male from Jeddah who had been reported as an asymptomatic infection from Alkhumra on 24 Jan 2016 at which time he was noted to have had direct exposure to camels. 6. On 10 Feb 2016 a 58-year-old non-healthcare worker Saudi male from Jeddah who had been reported as a new stable case on 22 Jan 2016 at which time he was noted to have had direct exposure to camels.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[These are the 1st newly reported cases since 3 Feb 2016, and represent the 10th and 11th reported infections with MERS-CoV infection since 1 Jan 2016. Two of the 11 reported cases this year (2016) were asymptomatic infections in individuals having contact with the camels in Alkhumra where there was an ongoing outbreak of MERS-CoV infection in the camels.Today's newly reported fatality is the 1st fatality reported since 18 Dec 2015 (when there was also a fatality reported in Najran).A map showing the locations of the newly reported cases can be found at the source URL above.The HealthMap/ProMED map of Saudi Arabia can be found at <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/131>. - Mod.MPP]
