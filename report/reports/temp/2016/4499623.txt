Dunya News [edited]<http://dunyanews.tv/en/Pakistan/353547-First-case-of-Congo-virus-surfaces-in-Faisalabad>The 1st Congo virus [Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever, CCHF] case has been reported in Faisalabad [Punjab] on [Sun 18 Sep 2016]. The 20-year-old patient RA was shifted in Civil hospital's private ward 3 days ago.Blood samples of the patient were sent to Islamabad where he was confirmed of having the disease. According to doctors, the condition of the patient is critical as the number of his platelets has decreased to 5000. Executive District Officer (EDO) health Nawazish Goraya confirmed the patient of having the disease.On the other hand, the death toll from Congo virus in Quetta [Balochistan] has reached 13 after 1 more patient died on [Sun 18 Sep 2016].Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever spreads through vectors that carry the virus found in cattle. The number of patients is rising as Eidul Azha [or Eid al-Adha, Festival of the Sacrifice] has prompted people to buy sacrificial animals.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[As mentioned, the increase in cases of Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever (CCHF) is expected following the Eid al-Adha Festival last week as cattle are a primary animal sacrificed for this festival.  This is now the 20th report on CCHF in Pakistan this year.  It appears as though cases have been reported from all provinces during 2016.The HealthMap/ProMED map of Punjab, Pakistan can be found at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/941> and of Balochistan, Pakistan: at <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/1299>. - Mod.MPP]
