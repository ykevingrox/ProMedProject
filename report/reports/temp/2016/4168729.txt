Xinhua [edited]<http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/2016-04/17/c_135287379.htm>Two human H7N9 cases have been confirmed in east China's Jiangxi Province, local health authorities said on Sunday [17 Apr 2016].The 2 patients are a 43 year old woman and a 23 year old man, a mother and son, said the provincial health and family planning commission. Both [patients] are natives of Taihe County in the city of Ji'an [Jiangxi Province]. They were working in Jinjiang City, Fujian Province before they found they had [become infected with] the disease. Then they went back to seek treatment in Jiangxi.They tested positive for H7N9 on Saturday [16 Apr 2016]. They are being treated in a hospital in Nanchang, capital of Jiangxi, and both are in a stable condition now, according to the commission.H7N9 is a bird flu strain 1st reported to have infected humans in March 2013 in China. It is most likely to strike in winter and spring.--communicated by:Ryan McGinnis<ryan@bigstormpicture.com>[ProMED-mail thanks Ryan McGinnis for contributing the report.Influenza A(H7N9) cases continue to be reported from China sporadically, with little evidence of human-to-human transmission.
