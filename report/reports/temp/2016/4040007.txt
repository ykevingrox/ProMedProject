Mod.NP, edited]<http://newsvo.ru/news/97976>In January 2016, 3 children in the Vologda region (in Veliky Ustyug and Sheksninsky districts and in Vologda city) fell ill with meningococcal infection; 2 of them died, said the regional Ministry of Health.In 2015, 7 cases of meningococcal disease were recorded in the Vologda region (1 case each in Babaev, Verhovazhsky, Vologda, and Veliky districts, and 3 cases in Vologda city); 5 of them were children; 1 of the 7 died, so mortality from meningococcal disease was 14 percent in 2015.--Communicated by:ProMED-RUS<promed-rus@promedmail.org>[The morbidity with generalized forms of meningococcal infection during the last 20 years varied within wide limits: from 0.2 to 5.0 per 100 thousand population in the different regions. Epidemics occur in cycles with peaks in incidence every 10-12 to 30 years. Currently in Russia, several polysaccharide vaccines against meningococcus A and C and meningococcal conjugate vaccine (serogroups A, C, Y and W-135) are registered. Vaccines against the meningococcus serogroup B are not available in Russia. Meningococcal vaccine coverage in Russia is, on average, 30-40 percent. - Mod.NPMaps of Russia can be seen at <http://www.mapsofworld.com/images/russia-political-enlarged-map.jpg> and <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/483>.Vologda Oblast, situated in Northwestern Russia on the Eastern European plain, with a population of 1.2 million people, is a federal subject of Russia (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vologda_Oblast#Geography>); it is divided into 4 cities and towns under the oblast's jurisdiction (Vologda, Cherepovets, Sokol, and Veliky Ustyug) and 26 districts. Its administrative center is the city of Vologda, with a population of 301 755 inhabitants in 2010 (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vologda>). - Mod.ML]
