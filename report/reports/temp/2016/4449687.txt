EpiCore Global Surveillance Project [edited]As per the news in various news paper & news in India as well as media alert by IDSP, the following information is available.The district administration on Thur [25 Aug 2016] ordered a probe to determine if there is an outbreak of a disease and whether it is the cause of all the deaths.Sukhveer Pahalwan, a Sarfabad resident and social activist said more than 100 people were suffering from fever in the village. ""There is panic among locals. The administration should quickly control the situation,"" he said. Residents allege poor sanitation in the village has led to the situation. Sector 73 and its nearby urbanscape have big housing societies. The victims are in the age group of 21-72 years.The health department in Gautam Budh Nagar said on Sat [27 Aug 2016] that 3 of the 12 deaths the villagers claim happened over the past 2 weeks were caused by fever, 2 of the patients died because of septicaemia while the rest succumbed to ""a combination of medical causes"".A report on 8 deaths was handed over to district magistrate NP Singh. ""We have handed over the report and it has been concluded that septicaemia was the cause in 2 cases, leading to multi-organ failure,"" chief medical officer Vijay Deepak Verma told TOI, a leading news paper in India.The team is yet to find a common cause for the deaths. ""We have found that some persons had a history of heart and liver ailments, pancreatitis and epilepsy. We have asked their families for a detailed medical history. The cases will then be analysed by senior doctors,"" said Deepak Kumar, chief medical officer, Gautam Budh Nagar. He added that blood samples of at least 50 residents have been sent for analysis but the reports do not show signs of any mosquitoborne or vectorborne disease. However the investigation is still ongoing and further information is awaited.--communicated by:ProMED-SoAs<promed-SoAs@promedmail.org>******[2] Date: 29 Aug 2016Source: EpiCore Global Surveillance Project [edited]Clinical findings indicate a viral cause for the febrile illness but the exact etiological agent has not been declared yet, even though various biological and environmental samples have been taken by the local health authorities.
