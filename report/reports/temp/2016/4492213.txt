On 9 Sep 2016, a 53-year old female was admitted to Sukraraj Tropical and Infectious Disease Hospital (STIDH) with a 9-day history of fever (on & off), severe joint pain (lower limbs followed by upper limbs), severe retro-orbital pain, severe headache (temporal region) and nausea. Serological test (IgM) for dengue was found positive. Dengue was confirmed by repeated testing. She was a resident of Chandanpur-1, Lalitpur district of Nepal. According to her, other people (relatives and neighbors) were also affected by similar signs and symptoms and are currently under treatment at different hospitals. To the best of my knowledge, this is the 1st reported case of dengue this year (2016) in Kathmandu, indicating that dengue is likely to start spreading in other places, since _Aedes aegypti_ and _Aedes albopictus_ are already well established in Kathmandu. Kathmandu has the highest population density in Nepal, and hence dengue must be considered while evaluating any individual with febrile illness, and campaign against dengue is urgently needed in order to prevent possible outbreaks of dengue in the coming days.Communicated by: Dr.Sher Bahadur Pun, MD, Clinical Research Unit, Sukraraj Tropical and Infectious Disease Hospital, Kathmandu, Nepal.<drsherbdr@yahoo.com>Sri Lanka- National. 28 Aug 2016.
