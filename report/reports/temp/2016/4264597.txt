Saudi Arabia Ministry of Health [edited]<http://www.moh.gov.sa/en/CCC/PressReleases/Pages/statistics-2016-06-3-001.aspx>As of 12:00 noon today [3 Jun 2016], there have been a total of:1384 laboratory-confirmed cases of MERS-CoV infection, including592 deaths [reported case fatality rate 42.8 percent]790 recoveries, and2 currently active casesInformation on newly reported case:- An 85-year-old Saudi female from Riyadh, non-healthcare worker, currently in stable condition. Classification is as a primary case, but history of exposure to camel is not known.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[The most recent reported case of MERS-CoV infection was reported by Saudi Arabia on 15 May 2016, in a 72-year-old Saudi male from Hail (see prior ProMED-mail post MERS-CoV (57): Saudi Arabia 20160515.4223430). In this interval from 15 May 2016 there have been an additional 2 recoveries reported, the 72-year-old Saudi male from Hail and a 48-year-old Saudi male from Hail.It appears as though this is now the ""quiet"" season for MERS-CoV transmission, and in the absence of nosocomial transmission, the occasional sporadic infection is being reported.
