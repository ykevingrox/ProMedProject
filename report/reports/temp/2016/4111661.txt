One of these reported cases is linked to the MERS-CoV outbreak currently occurring in a hospital in Buraidah city.Details of the cases--------------------1. A 47 year old male from Taif city developed symptoms on [9 Mar 2016] and, on [10 Mar 2016], was admitted to hospital. The patient, who has no comorbidities, tested positive for MERS-CoV on [11 Mar 2016]. Currently, he is in a stable condition in a negative pressure isolation room on a ward. The patient has a history of contact with a previously reported MERS-CoV case (see DON published on [16 Mar 2016] -- case no. 1). He was under observation and was identified after developing symptoms. The patient has no history of exposure to the other known risk factors in the 14 days prior to the onset of symptoms [reported as a newly confirmed case on 14 Mar 2016 at which time he was noted to be in a stable condition. At the time of initial confirmation he was classified as a secondary case in a household contact. - Mod.MPP]2. A 45 year old male from Alkharj city developed symptoms on [11 Mar 2016] and was admitted to hospital on [13 Mar 2016]. The patient, who has comorbidities, tested positive for MERS-CoV on [14 Mar 2016]. Currently, he is in a critical condition in ICU. The patient has a history of frequent contact with camels and consumption of their raw milk. He has no history of exposure to the other known risk factors in the 14 days prior to the onset of symptoms. The Ministry of Agriculture was notified and investigation of camels is ongoing [reported as a newly confirmed case on 14 Mar 2016 at which time he was noted to be in critical condition. He was reported as a fatality on 18 Mar 2016. At the time of initial confirmation report, he was classified as a primary case with a history of contact with camels in the 14 days preceding onset of illness. - Mod.MPP]3. A 60 year old male from Hail city developed fever, nausea, and shortness of breath on [5 Mar 2016] and, on [10 Mar 2016], was admitted to the hospital in Buraidah where the MERS outbreak is currently occurring. The patient, who has comorbidities, tested positive for MERS-CoV on [12 Mar 2016]. Currently, the patient is in a critical condition in ICU. Exposure to the known risk factors in the 14 days prior to the onset of symptoms is being investigated [reported as a newly confirmed case on 13 Mar 2016, when he was noted to be in a critical condition. He was reported as a fatality on 15 Mar 2016. A 33 year old, non-national male from Buraidah city developed symptoms on [2 Mar 2016] and on [10 Mar 2016] was admitted to the hospital where the MERS outbreak is currently occurring. The patient, who has no comorbidities, tested positive for MERS-CoV on [12 Mar 2016]. Currently, he is in a critical condition in ICU. The patient frequently visited a family member who has been an inpatient in the same hospital since [23 Feb 2016] for an unrelated medical condition.
