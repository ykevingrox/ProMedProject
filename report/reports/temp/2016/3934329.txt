Burma News International (BNI) Online, Kalandan Press report [edited]<http://www.bnionline.net/news/kaladan-press/item/1417-pneumonia-on-rise-in-maungdaw.html>Cases of pneumonia and fever have been rising in Maungdaw South [Rakhine State] since colder weather arrived on [1 Jan 2016], according to a Rohingya [Indo-Aryan peoples from the Rakhine State] from the area. He said that most of the affected people are elderly villagers and children under the age of 5 [years].Two women, [one aged 60 years and one aged 55 years] died on 2 January [2016], in Khonzabill village in Aley Than Kyaw Village Tract, Maungdaw, after catching pneumonia and suffering from a cough and fever, according to one of their relatives.[Four others, aged 50 years, 75 years, 45 years, and 40 years,] of Khonzabill Village have also been suffering from fever. They were taken to of Aley Than Kyaw Hospital, but they did not receive proper treatment and were only given some tablets and sent back home the same day, according to a staff member at the hospital who did not want to be identified. He also said that at present many people in the area, including women and children, are suffering from pneumonia, fevers, and coughs.An elder from the area who did not give his name said that the government has not been taking any action against the spread of pneumonia.[Byline: Mark Inkey]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[Maps of Myanmar can be seen at <http://www.nationsonline.org/oneworld/map/myanmar_map2.htm> and <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/10628>.Maungdaw is a district of the coastal Rakhine State in western Myanmar located on the Bay of Bengal (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maungdaw_District>) bordered by Bangladesh to the northwest. The town of Maungdaw, with a population of 400 000 in 2008, is the westernmost city of Myanmar and borders Bangladesh; the majority of the populace, about 80 percent, are said to be stateless Muslims of the Rohingya minority group (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maungdaw>).
