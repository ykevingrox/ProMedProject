myRepublica [edited]<http://www.myrepublica.com/news/7173>As many as 59 new cases of scrub typhus infection have been reported in Chitwan district in Asoj [6th month of the year in Nepali calendar that corresponds to 17 Sep to 17 Oct], pushing the number of patients in the district to 264 in the last 6 months.According to the insect control inspector at the District Public Health, Chitwan, Ram Kumar KC, this bacterial disease has already claimed 2 lives in the district.A total of 8 people have been reported dead due to the infection since its outbreak in the eastern part of the country, said Resham Lamicchane, public health officer at the Epidemiology and Disease Control Division of the Ministry of Health. According to him, scrub typhus cases have been reported from 37 districts [out of a total of 75 districts].--communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[When last reported in a ProMED-mail post there were 162 cases of scrub typhus reported in the 4 months from May to August 2016 in the Chitwan district. There are now 264 in the last 6 months, with 2 fatalities in this district and cases of scrub typhus cases being reported from 37 districts out of a total of 75 districts in Nepal.Chitwan is a district in Central Region of Nepal.
