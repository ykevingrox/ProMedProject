Times of India [edited]<http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/coimbatore/Sainik-School-closed-after-one-student-dies-24-fall-ill/articleshow/53236701.cms>A Class 6 student of Sainik School at Amaravathi Nagar at Udumalpet in Tirupur district [Tamil Nadu state], who had been suffering from severe fever since Thursday night [14 Jul 2016], died on the school campus on Friday morning [15 Jul 2016]. Some 24 other students of the school were admitted to the government hospital at Udumalpet as they were also suffering from fever.The parents of the students staged a road roko at Udumalpet on Friday evening and traffic was affected for more than one hour on Palani-Pollachi road. [A roko is a ""protest or demonstration, typically one that involves the obstruction of a railway or road"" (<http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/us/definition/american_english/roko>).] The school principal Captain T N Sridhar has announced holiday for the school till 24 Jul [2016].The boy who died was 11 [years old], from Thirunagar at Palani in Dindigul district [Tamil Nadu state]. Police said the boy joined the school only 15 days ago. On Friday around 7.30am, he had gone to the cadet's mess at the school and tried to eat roti and milk. However, he vomited and fainted. He was rushed to the Udumalpet government hospital staff where doctors declared him as brought dead.Immediately the school authorities informed parents of [the dead boy] who rushed to the hospital. They said they were not informed by the school authorities when their son was suffering from fever. Only after his death, were they informed, they said. Meanwhile 7 students from Class 6, 15 students from Class 8, 1 student from Class 10 and 1 from Class 12 were admitted at the Udumalpet government hospital. ""We have taken some blood samples from the students and sent to the lab for further test,"" said a doctor.Over 100 people, mostly women who were relatives of the deceased [boy] and parents of the other students, staged a road roko for one hour on the Palani-Pollachi road, demanding action against the school authorities.
