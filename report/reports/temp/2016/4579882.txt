The Times of India, Times News Network (TNN) [edited]<http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/bhubaneswar/JE-toll-rises-to-68-in-Malkangiri/articleshow/55008338.cms>The unofficial death toll due to Japanese encephalitis [JE] touched 68 with the death of a 4-year old girl at the Malkangiri district [Odisha] headquarters hospital late on [Fri 21 Oct 2016].Akash Srivastava, the joint director (Epidemiology), National Centre for Disease Control, who is in Malkangiri, said the administration has taken many steps to contain the disease. The central team has also collected blood samples from several suspected JE affected children for laboratory tests, said Srivastava, who is heading the team of experts.The team visited few of the JE-affected villages. It will submit one copy each of the report to the state health department and to director, NVBDCP [National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme], New Delhi. On [Sat 22 Oct 2016], a new JE case was admitted to the hospital.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[The number of JE fatalities continues to mount, increasing from 59 on 18 Oct 2016 to 68 (as of 23 Oct 2016). However, no mention is made if these cases diagnosed as JE virus infections are laboratory confirmed. Blood samples have been collected for testing, and ProMED would be interested in the results confirming or eliminating JE virus as the etiological agent involved in these cases, as well as the measures being taken to contain the disease.
