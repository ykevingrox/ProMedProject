The individual will join an international team of over 40 global health specialists located in 32 countries. Coverage of events outside the region but with potential importance to the region is also expected. The infectious diseases moderator will work closely with the ProMED-EAFR regional moderator, other EAFR moderators, and the ProMED-mail deputy editor.Key responsibilities and duties-------------------------------Reporting:1. Check email at least twice daily, morning and afternoon/evening Monday through Friday, and at least once a day during weekends and holidays.2. Actively and regularly search the internet -- including the world wide web, local media, disease outbreak sources of information (such as HealthMap), social media platforms, and relevant blogs -- for reports of outbreaks of emerging infectious diseases in the region.3. Using an online dashboard tool, post relevant reports of events of interest in human/animal health to the EAFR region with appropriate commentary, in a timely manner (less than 24 hours). Reports should include short, knowledgeable, appropriate contextual commentaries with background information.Subscriptions:4. Find relevant regional and national email lists and websites of universities and professional societies, to which you can post an invitation to subscribe to ProMED-EAFR.5. Assist in recruitment of new subscribers and outreach at local and regional professional conferences and meetings.6. Respond to requests from subscribers.Inter- and intra- staff:7. Assist in training of new staff.8. Attend periodic meetings (annual or biannual) of ProMED-mail staff.9. Report to the ProMED-mail project manager and deputy editor an informal monthly report, and a formal quarterly report of your activities, specifying the number of outbreak items you've found in your region; the number of ProMED-EAFR messages you moderated; and the number of invitations to join ProMED-EAFR you forwarded to relevant individuals or lists; plus any other information you believe to be of interest to ProMED-EAFR.10. Set a regular schedule with the regional moderator and ProMED-mail deputy editor and notify him/her in advance of any expected absence from moderation duties.11. Contact the regional moderator with any questions or suggestions for improving ProMED-EAFR.Qualifications--------------1. An advanced degree in the human/animal health field required (MD, MBBS, DVM, PhD in microbiology or similar field). Additional advanced degree in a related field preferred (MPH or participation in an international Field Epidemiology Training Program).2. Five or more years of experience in human or animal infectious diseases, specifically in diagnosis, treatment, and/or research.3. A track record of achievement in academic, governmental or nongovernmental organization, commensurate with career stage.4. Ability and interest in maintaining expertise in infectious diseases through continuing education.5. Email and world wide web proficiency and daily access.6. Familiarity with ProMED-mail archives.7. Nationality of one of the countries in the EAFR region required.Interested individuals should forward a cover letter summarizing interests and qualifications and curriculum vitae to <positions@isid.org>, with the subject line: Moderator, ProMED-EAFR.
