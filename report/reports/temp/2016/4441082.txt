NH1.com [edited]<http://www.nh1.com/news/gonorrhea-on-the-rise-67-percent-in-nh-especially-in-1-county/>Gonorrhea is on the rise in New Hampshire, with a 67 percent increase of cases in 2016 compared to 2015. So far, 206 cases have been reported between 1 Jan 2016 and 31 Jul 2016, according to a medical alert sent by the Division of Public Health Services Bureau of Infection Disease Control.Those who have been the most impacted by the sexually transmitted disease have been men under 40 years old and living in Hillsborough County. Merrimack, Rockingham, and Strafford counties have also had a rise in reported cases.The CDC recommends that gonorrhea gets treated with a shot of ceftriaxone and azithromycin taken orally. The dual therapy has remained effective even though the Gonococcal Isolate Surveillance Project studies show that gonorrhea is becoming more resistant to the antimicrobial azithromycin.""For all of your patients, take a thorough sexual history to determine patient risk and need for STD screening,"" the department said.[Byline: Brooke Coupal]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail<promed@promedmail.org>[Of interest, in the 2014 CDC statistics on the incidence of gonorrhea in the USA <http://www.cdc.gov/std/stats14/tables/13.htm>, the state of New Hampshire was number 49 out of 50 American states with an incidence of 17.1 cases per 100 000 population. The state of Louisiana was number 1 at 194.6 cases per 100 000 and the overall USA rate was 110.7 per 100 000. It is not stated if the cases were in higher risk groups such as illicit drug users or men who have sex with men. - Mod.LL]
