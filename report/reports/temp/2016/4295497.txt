The Northeast Today (TNT) [edited]<http://thenortheasttoday.com/japanese-encephalitis-and-dengue-claim-6-leave-200-affected/>Japanese encephalitis seems to have taken an even more serious turn at Lahorighat and Bhurbandha blocks of Morigaon district [Assam state], claiming 6 lives and affecting more than 200 people of several villages so far.As per a statement given by Dr Biplab Kumar Gohain, Joint Director of Health, Morigaon, 4 such death cases have occurred due to Japanese encephalitis in the district in the past 48 hours. It was informed that at Lahorighat Tengaguri village, a 65-year-old person and a 9-year-old boy died of the disease. Their death reports confirmed infection by the acute encephalitis virus [sic. Northeastern India often reports cases of acute encephalitis syndrome (AES). Sometimes the etiological agent is Japanese encephalitis virus, as is the situation here, but frequently the etiology is undetermined. - Mod.TY][A 55-year-old person] of Balijan village and another of Solmari village have also lost their lives to this disease.
