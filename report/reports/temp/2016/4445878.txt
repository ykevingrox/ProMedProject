International Business Times [edited]<http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/south-korea-finds-second-case-cholera-one-week-1577904>South Korea finds 2nd case of cholera in one week-------------------------------------------------Health authorities in South Korea on Thursday (24 Aug 2016) confirmed the 2nd case of cholera in the country. A 73-year-old woman has been found contaminated with the water-borne disease, according to reports.The South Gyeongsang Provincial Government said that the woman -- a resident of the southern coast of the country -- got the infection after eating seafood at a local restaurant.[Byline: Divya Kishore]--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[The name of the province (<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Gyeongsang_Province>) derives from Korean Gyeongsang, meaning ""joyous furthermore"". The name derives from the names of the principal cities of Gyeongju and Sangju.According to the GIDEON database (and as stated in ProMED-mail post Cholera - South Korea 20010906.2138), the Republic of South Korea reported 1964 cases during 1950 to 1969; 475 in 1970; 145 in 1980; and 0 during 1984 to 1988. There were 6 imported cases reported in 1992; 5 (imported, 0 fatal) in 1993; 34 (imported, 0 fatal) in 1994; 74 (6 imported, 0 fatal) in 1995; 7 (5 imported, 0 fatal) in 1996; 10 (0 fatal) in 1997. The 2001 cholera outbreak in South Korea affected at least 137 people. - Mod.LLA HealthMap/ProMED-mail map can be accessed at: <http://healthmap.org/promed/p/15657>.]
