.0,6580450.0,2016-07-28 20:18:15,"Udayapur, Eastern Region, Nepal",Nepal,Scrub Typhus,Humans,?id=20160728.4377598,PRO/AH/EDR> Scrub typhus - Nepal: (UD),"SCRUB TYPHUS - NEPAL: (UDAYAPUR)********************************A ProMED-mail post<http://www.promedmail.org>ProMED-mail is a program of theInternational Society for Infectious Diseases<http://www.isid.org>Date: Thu 28 Jul 2016From: Dr. Sher Bahadur Pun <drsherbdr@yahoo.com> [edited]On 8 Jul 2016, a 59-year-old female was admitted to Sukraraj Tropical and Infectious Disease Hospital (STIDH) with a 2-week history of high-grade fever with chills, red eyes, joint pain, body ache, headache and loss of appetite. She had, however, no history of rashes or eschar. Abnormal liver function tests were found in the routine laboratory test. Serological tests for leptospirosis, dengue fever and typhoid fever were found negative.However, ELISA was tested positive for scrub typhus. She was treated with doxycycline and recovered without complications.The patient was a resident of Gaighat, Udayapur district of Sagarmatha Zone, Eastern region of Nepal. In 2015, Epidemiology and Disease Control Division (EDCD) had informed that scrub typhus cases were reported from Sankhuwasabha, Bhojpur, Siraha, Khotang, Dhading, Doti, Dadeldhura, Darchula and Kailali districts. To the best of my knowledge, this is the 1st reported case of scrub typhus from Udayapur district of Nepal, indicating that scrub typhus may have continued to spread to other parts (districts) of the country.--Dr. Sher Bahadur Pun, MD, PhDClinical Research UnitSukraraj Tropical and Infectious Disease HospitalKathmandu, Nepal<drsherbdr@yahoo.com>[ProMED-mail thanks Dr Sher Bahadur Pun for continued contributions to ProMED-mail, by providing this 1st report of confirmed scrub typhus in Udayapur district (one of the 75 districts of Nepal), in the Sagarmatha Zone, Eastern region of Nepal.The patient's town of Gaighat can be found on a map of the Udayapur district at <http://ncthakur.itgo.com/districtmaps/udayapur_district.htm>.
