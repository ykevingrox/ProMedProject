The Poultry Site [edited]<http://www.thepoultrysite.com/poultrynews/36675/more-bird-flu-found-in>Another outbreak of highly pathogenic avian influenza has been discovered in village poultry in Viet Nam.Over 700 birds died and a further 300 were destroyed as a result of the H5N1 outbreak in Tra Vinh province.Viet Nam is using vaccination as a measure to try and contain the disease.--Communicated by:ProMED-mail from HealthMap Alerts<promed@promedmail.org>[The HPAI H5N1 outbreak in Tra Vinh mentioned above was reported to the OIE on 26 Feb 2016 in follow-up report no. 6, available with an interactive map at <http://www.oie.int/wahis_2/public/wahid.php/Reviewreport/Review?page_refer=MapFullEventReport&reportid=19808>.This continuing H5N1 event started on 7 Nov 2015 and was summarized, as of 26 Feb 2016, as follows:Total outbreaks = 5 (Submitted)Species / Susceptible / Cases / Deaths / Destroyed / SlaughteredBirds / 44 869 / 3889 /3110 / 41759 / 0Viet Nam applies stamping out policy in relation to HPAI. Since the start of this event, the ""measures applied"" section of the OIE reports (an immediate notification on 17 Nov 2015 and 5 follow-up reports); links to all at <http://www.oie.int/wahis_2/public/wahid.php/Reviewreport/Review/viewsummary?fupser=&dothis=&reportid=19102>  indicated ""no vaccination"".
